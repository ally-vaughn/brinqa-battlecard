    Brinqa — The Full Story

    Brinqa is not a vulnerability scanner — it is a unified exposure management and risk orchestration platform that sits above your existing security stack. Brinqa ingests findings from every scanner, cloud and identity tool, application security platform, pen test, and threat intelligence feed you already own, then normalizes them into a single Cyber Risk Graph and long-term data layer with consistent, AI-enhanced scoring. That trusted data foundation lets Brinqa reconcile conflicting signals, fill ownership gaps, and route deduplicated, prioritized exposure records to the right IT, cloud, and app teams through automated workflows and existing ITSM tools. The result: fewer critical exposures left open, faster and more predictable remediation SLAs, and defensible, business-level risk narratives your CISO can confidently present to the board.

    Objection Handling
    "We already use Tenable — why do we need Brinqa on top?"
    Tenable gives you the findings. Brinqa gives you the program. If your team is still manually triaging CVEs, creating ITSM tickets by hand, and struggling to show the board how risk is trending in business terms, that's the gap Brinqa fills. We're not replacing Tenable — we're the orchestration and CRQ layer that makes your Tenable investment 3× more actionable. Ask: how long does it take a Tenable finding to become a closed, verified remediation ticket today?
    "Tenable One includes exposure management — isn't that enough?"
    Tenable One is an excellent platform for organizations that standardize on the Tenable ecosystem. If your entire scanner fleet is Tenable, their exposure management story is coherent. The question is your stack reality: if you also run Qualys for one environment, Wiz for cloud, and receive manual pen test findings from a third party, Tenable One can't normalize those into a consistent risk view. Brinqa is the answer to that multi-source reality.
    "Tenable has VPR — that's already risk-based prioritization."
    VPR scores vulnerabilities in isolation against threat intelligence. Brinqa applies your business context on top — asset criticality, data sensitivity, compensating controls already in place, regulatory scope, ownership assignment — to produce a risk score that reflects your actual environment. Two identical CVEs on different assets should have different remediation priority based on what's behind them. That contextual layer is what VPR alone cannot provide.
    "Tenable is easier to use and deploy — our team is stretched thin."
    That's a fair point and it's true: Tenable wins on deployment simplicity and day-one usability. The trade-off is customization and scope. If your environment is relatively homogeneous and Tenable covers your primary scanning need, the simplicity argument holds. If you have a complex multi-tool environment with 5+ data sources, ownership gaps, and board reporting requirements, the upfront investment in Brinqa's configuration pays off in a program you can actually sustain and scale.

Brinqa aggregates from cloud security tools and normalizes their findings into the same risk register as traditional infrastructure data, with richer prioritization context than Ivanti's primarily scan-and-patch workflow model.
    Where Ivanti Wins
    • Broader IT/Security Platform with Operational Depth: Ivanti is a larger platform vendor with broader reach across IT operations — patch management, ITSM, CMDB, asset management, and RBVM in one suite. For organizations that value operational integration and a broader suite relationship over deep exposure-model customization, Ivanti is often easier to justify internally: it extends a platform the IT team already owns and trusts (PeerSpot 2025).
    • Native Patch + Remediation Loop: Ivanti Neurons for RBVM natively integrates with Neurons Patch Management, creating a closed loop from vulnerability detection to automated patch deployment. For organizations already on the Ivanti platform, this single-vendor patch-to-close workflow is genuinely simpler than multi-tool orchestration.
    • ITSM-Native Workflow: For organizations running Ivanti Neurons ITSM, the RBVM workflow integration is tight — vulnerabilities automatically generate ITSM tickets with asset ownership, risk context, and SLA assignments within the same platform. This is a compelling operational simplification for existing Ivanti customers.
    • Ransomware-Focused Dashboards: Ivanti's ransomware-specific prioritization view — highlighting vulnerabilities actively exploited in ransomware campaigns — is a targeted feature that resonates in conversations focused on immediate threat-specific risk reduction.
    Objection Handling
    "We already use Ivanti for patch management — adding RBVM is a natural extension."
    The extension logic is sound if your entire IT operations stack is Ivanti. The question is scope: Ivanti RBVM is strongest when the remediation path ends in an Ivanti patch. For vulnerabilities in cloud workloads, third-party applications, or non-patchable configurations, the Ivanti workflow breaks. Brinqa handles the full remediation orchestration across teams and tools — including sending work orders back into Ivanti's ITSM where that's the right destination.
    "Ivanti gives us RBVM as part of a broader IT platform — it's easier to justify than a point solution."
    That's a legitimate procurement argument for organizations optimizing for operational simplicity and IT platform consolidation. Brinqa's buying case is different: if your security problem is fragmented risk data — many scanners, inconsistent ownership, no unified exposure model — then a single-platform IT suite doesn't solve that. Brinqa is the specialist exposure intelligence layer on top of your existing stack, including Ivanti. The two roles are complementary, not competing.
    "Ivanti's ransomware dashboard shows us what to patch first — that's our priority use case."
    Ransomware-specific prioritization is valuable and Ivanti does it well for patchable vulnerabilities in its coverage scope. Brinqa extends that prioritization across the full attack surface — including cloud exposure paths and misconfigurations that Ivanti's scanner doesn't see. When ransomware actors exploit non-patchable misconfigurations or cloud identity gaps, Brinqa's broader aggregation is the safety net.

    "We just want to focus on the vulnerabilities that are actually exploitable — not build a broad data layer."
    That's exactly Zafran's use case and it's a valid starting point. The question is whether exploitability filtering alone constitutes an enterprise exposure program.     "Zafran can tell us what's actually exploitable — that's a huge reduction in noise."
    Exploitability context is genuinely valuable and Zafran's approach is sound. The question is what happens after the filter: who routes the remaining high-priority findings to the right team, with SLA accountability, and produces a board-ready risk number? Brinqa takes the prioritized signal — including Zafran's output — and turns it into a closed-loop remediation program with executive reporting. The two are complementary, not competing.
    "It's part of CrowdStrike now — that gives us confidence in the roadmap."
    If your organization is already a full CrowdStrike shop, the integration story is compelling. If you run a multi-vendor stack — Tenable for scanning, Wiz for cloud, Rapid7 for SIEM — the question is whether Zafran's roadmap continues to serve non-Falcon environments or whether feature investment migrates toward Falcon-native capabilities as the acquisition matures. That's a real strategic risk worth pricing into a multi-year evaluation.
